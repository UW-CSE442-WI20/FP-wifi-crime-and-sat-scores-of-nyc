A story to tell


Here we have a visualization of the total noise compaints in each New York City borough from the year 2014-2015. Hover over each section of circle to view the number of noise complaints from that borough. The more noise complaints, the bigger the circle. As we move on in this journey, think about how the noise complaints may affect SAT scores of high schools in each bourough. More noise complaints likely means more parties. More partying means less studying. Less studying likely leads to lower SAT scores... right? Seems logical, but only the data will truly tell. We'd also be fools to ignore the fact that each borough is a different size, so even though there are a lot more noise complaints in an area, it could also be much bigger. What may be more relevant and helpful to draw connections from is the noise complaint density in each borough...

We reveal the true, more relative circle sizes based off the noise complaint density. It is interesting to note how the circle sizes change once square mileage is factored into the picture. While Brooklyn has the most incidents of reported noise complaints, it turns out that Manhattan actually has the highest number of complaints per square mile. It originally had nearly the same amount of complaints as Brooklyn, but it's size is a fraction of that of Brooklyn. The opposite goes for Queens.

In the final section of this story, I encourage you to think make other connections in addition to just the SAT scores. How might the level of noise complaints have a relationship with crime, income, and even WiFi density? Move along and explore for yourself.
